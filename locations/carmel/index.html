What We Do in Carmel

Full-Service Lawn Care & Landscaping

Whether you need weekly mowing or a complete landscape overhaul, we handle it. Here's a look at our most popular services for Carmel properties.

Lawn Mowing & Maintenance

We're on your Carmel property the same day every week from mid-April through November. Every visit includes mowing with alternating patterns, edging along all sidewalks and driveways, string trimming, and blowing. We use commercial-grade equipment with freshly sharpened blades and follow the one-third rule to keep your turf thick and healthy. Learn more about our mowing service.

Fertilization & Weed Control

Carmel lawns deal with heavy weed pressure from crabgrass, dandelions, and clover, especially in neighborhoods with mature trees where the turf thins out in shaded areas. Our fertilization programs include pre-emergent applications timed for Hamilton County's spring soil temperatures, targeted post-emergent treatments throughout the season, and a winterizer application that strengthens root systems before the ground freezes.

Aeration & Overseeding

Many Carmel neighborhoods were built on compacted clay fill, and years of foot traffic and mowing only make it worse. Fall core aeration breaks through that compaction so water, air, and nutrients can reach the root zone. Overseeding fills in thin and bare areas, and by the following spring your lawn comes back noticeably thicker.

Landscaping, Mulch & Everything Else

Curb appeal matters in Carmel, and we help you keep it sharp. Mulch and rock installation, landscape bed renovations, shrub and hedge trimming, paver patios and driveways, seasonal flower plantings, spring and fall cleanups, leaf removal, and snow removal. Full-service, twelve months a year.

Your Year-Round Plan

What Your Carmel Property Needs, Season by Season

Carmel properties face specific seasonal demands. Here's what we handle for our Carmel customers through each phase of the year.

Spring (March through May)

Spring cleanup to clear winter damage and re-edge beds along the Monon Trail properties and throughout the city. Pre-emergent weed control timed for Carmel's soil temperatures. Fresh mulch in landscape beds. Spring annuals at entrances for immediate color. First mow typically the third week of April.

Summer (June through August)

Weekly mowing at proper height. Post-emergent weed control for the crabgrass and clover that pushes hardest in Carmel's mature, partially shaded neighborhoods. Shrub and hedge trimming to maintain the curb appeal standards Carmel is known for. Mosquito control for properties near Cool Creek and retention areas. Monitor for fungal disease in shaded, humid spots.

Fall (September through November)

Core aeration and overseeding in early fall. Winterizer fertilizer. Heavy leaf removal under Carmel's mature tree canopy, especially in the established Brookshire, Woodland Springs, and Home Place neighborhoods. Fall cleanup of beds and property.

Winter (December through February)

Commercial snow removal and ice management for parking lots and commercial properties. Priority response for commercial clients along the Meridian Street and Rangeline Road corridors. Spring project planning.
