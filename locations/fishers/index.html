What We Do in Fishers

Full-Service Lawn Care & Landscaping

From weekly mowing to complete landscape overhauls, we keep Fishers properties looking their best year-round. Here's what we do most.

Lawn Mowing & Maintenance

Same day, every week, mid-April through November. Mowing with alternating patterns, edging along every sidewalk and driveway, string trimming around every obstacle, and blowing all clippings off hard surfaces. We use commercial-grade mowers with sharp blades and follow the one-third rule to keep your turf healthy and thick all season. Learn more about our mowing service.

Fertilization & Weed Control

Fishers lawns face the same Hamilton County challenges: clay-heavy soil, aggressive crabgrass pressure in summer, and dandelion invasions in spring and fall. Our fertilization programs are timed for central Indiana's conditions, not a generic national schedule. That means pre-emergent applications when soil temps hit the right threshold, targeted post-emergent weed treatments through the season, and a winterizer to build root strength before the ground freezes.

Aeration & Overseeding

Compacted soil is the silent problem behind most thin, struggling Fishers lawns. Fall core aeration breaks through that compaction layer so water, air, and nutrients can reach the root zone. Overseeding fills in bare and thin spots, and by the following spring your lawn is noticeably thicker. If you only invest in one extra service beyond mowing, this is the one.

Landscaping, Mulch & Everything Else

Mulch and rock installation, landscape bed renovations, hedge and shrub trimming, paver patios and driveways, seasonal flower plantings, spring and fall cleanups, leaf removal, mosquito control, and snow removal. Full-service care, twelve months a year.

Your Year-Round Plan

What Your Fishers Property Needs, Season by Season

Fishers properties range from compact Saxony-area yards to multi-acre lots near Geist. Here's how we keep them all looking sharp through every season.

Spring (March through May)

Spring cleanup to clear winter debris and prep beds. Pre-emergent weed control timed for Fishers soil temps. Fresh mulch to refresh landscape beds. Spring annuals for entrances and focal points. Flea and tick treatments start in April as temperatures climb, especially important for properties bordering wooded areas near Geist.

Summer (June through August)

Weekly mowing at proper height. Ongoing weed control. Mosquito control is essential for Geist Reservoir-area properties and neighborhoods with retention ponds. Shrub trimming throughout the season. Disease monitoring during humid stretches. Chinch bug watch in sunny areas near driveways and sidewalks.

Fall (September through November)

Core aeration and overseeding for thicker turf next year. Winterizer fertilizer. Leaf removal, which can be heavy in the older Fishers neighborhoods with mature hardwoods near 116th Street and around Geist. Fall cleanup to clear beds and prepare for winter.

Winter (December through February)

Commercial snow removal for commercial properties along the 116th Street and Allisonville Road corridors. Spring planning for landscape projects and paver installations.
