What We Do in Westfield

Full-Service Lawn Care & Landscaping

From establishing a brand-new lawn to maintaining a mature property year-round, we handle it all. Here's what we do most in Westfield.

Lawn Mowing & Maintenance

We're on your Westfield property the same day every week from mid-April through November. Mowing, edging, string trimming, and blowing, every visit. We alternate mowing patterns to prevent ruts, keep our blades sharp for a clean cut, and follow the one-third rule so your grass stays healthy through the season. Learn more about our mowing service.

Fertilization & Weed Control

New construction lawns in Westfield often struggle because the topsoil was stripped or compacted during grading, leaving thin turf that weeds love to invade. Our fertilization programs are built to address that, starting with properly timed pre-emergent applications and continuing through the season with targeted weed treatments and a winterizer application to strengthen roots before dormancy.

Aeration, Overseeding & New Lawn Establishment

If you moved into a new Westfield home and the builder-grade lawn looks thin, patchy, or weed-ridden, you're not alone. Fall core aeration and overseeding is the fastest way to thicken up that turf. For properties starting from scratch, we also offer new lawn seeding and sod installation to get you started right.

Landscaping, Mulch & Everything Else

Mulch and rock installation, landscape bed renovations, hedge and shrub trimming, paver patios and driveways, seasonal flower plantings, spring and fall cleanups, leaf removal, and snow removal. We take care of your outdoor property twelve months a year.

Your Year-Round Plan

What Your Westfield Property Needs, Season by Season

New-construction lawns and established properties have different needs at different times of year. Here's what we handle for Westfield customers through each season.

Spring (March through May)

Spring cleanup to clear winter debris and reshape bed edges. Pre-emergent weed control is critical in Westfield's newer developments where thin turf is especially vulnerable to crabgrass invasion. Fresh mulch in landscape beds. For new-construction homes without established landscaping, spring is a great time to plan bed buildouts and new lawn seeding or sod.

Summer (June through August)

Weekly mowing at 3.75 to 4 inches. Continued weed treatments as summer annuals push through. Trimming and pruning to keep new and established landscaping shaped. Mosquito control for properties near the retention ponds that are common in Westfield's newer subdivisions. Chinch bug monitoring in the hotter, sunnier yards along the SR 32 corridor.

Fall (September through November)

The most important season. Core aeration and overseeding to thicken turf, especially in the newer developments where builder-grade lawns are still thin. Winterizer fertilizer. Leaf removal and fall cleanup to prep the property for winter.

Winter (December through February)

Commercial snow removal for commercial properties around Grand Junction and the 161st Street commercial corridor. Planning season for spring landscape projects.
