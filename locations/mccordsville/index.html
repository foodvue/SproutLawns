What We Do in McCordsville

Full-Service Lawn Care & Landscaping

Whether your home is brand new or you've been here for years, we handle every aspect of your outdoor property. Here's what McCordsville homeowners rely on us for most.

Lawn Mowing & Maintenance

We're on your McCordsville property the same day every week from mid-April through November. Mowing with alternating patterns, edging along all sidewalks and driveways, string trimming, and blowing. Every visit, no exceptions. We follow the one-third rule and keep our blades sharp for a clean cut that keeps your turf healthy. Learn more about our mowing service.

Fertilization & Weed Control

New-construction lawns in McCordsville are notorious for thin turf on compacted subsoil. Weeds love those conditions. Our fertilization and weed control program builds soil fertility back up with properly timed applications while keeping crabgrass, dandelions, and clover in check throughout the growing season.

Aeration, Overseeding & New Lawn Establishment

If your builder-grade lawn looks patchy and thin after a year or two, it's not your fault. The topsoil was likely stripped during construction, and what's left is compacted clay. Fall core aeration and overseeding is the single best thing you can do to turn it around. For yards starting from bare dirt, we also offer new lawn seeding and sod installation.

Landscaping, Mulch & Everything Else

Mulch and rock installation, landscape bed renovations, hedge and shrub trimming, paver patios, seasonal flower plantings, spring and fall cleanups, leaf removal, mosquito control, and snow removal. Full-service, twelve months a year.

Your Year-Round Plan

What Your McCordsville Property Needs, Season by Season

New-construction lawns dominate McCordsville right now, and they have specific seasonal needs. Here's how we keep McCordsville properties on track.

Spring (March through May)

Spring cleanup to clear whatever winter left behind. Pre-emergent weed control is especially important on newer McCordsville lawns where thin turf gives weeds an easy foothold. Fresh mulch in beds. For new construction homes that still need landscaping, spring is the ideal time for bed buildouts, sod installation, and new lawn seeding.

Summer (June through August)

Weekly mowing at 3.75 to 4 inches to keep turf healthy through the heat. Ongoing weed control. Mosquito control for properties near the retention ponds found in most McCordsville subdivisions. Flea and tick treatments for properties backing up to fields or tree lines.

Fall (September through November)

Core aeration and overseeding to thicken up builder-grade lawns. This is the single most impactful service for new-construction McCordsville properties. Winterizer fertilizer. Leaf removal and fall cleanup.

Winter (December through February)

Commercial snow removal for commercial properties along the SR 67 corridor. Planning for spring landscape installations.
