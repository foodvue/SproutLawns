What We Do in Noblesville

Full-Service Lawn Care & Landscaping

From the first mow in April to the last snow push in March, we take care of your property so you don't have to think about it. Here's what that looks like.

Lawn Mowing & Maintenance

Our bread and butter. We're on your Noblesville property the same day every week from mid-April through November. Mowing, edging, trimming, and blowing. We alternate mowing patterns to prevent ruts, sharpen blades on a strict schedule for a clean cut, and follow the one-third rule so your grass stays thick and healthy all season. Learn more about our mowing service.

Fertilization & Weed Control

Noblesville soil is predominantly clay-heavy, which means it holds nutrients unevenly and compacts easily. Our fertilization programs are built around that reality. Not a one-size-fits-all schedule from a national chain. We test nutrient levels and apply the right pre-emergent and post-emergent treatments at the right time, including a winterizer application that sets your lawn up for a stronger spring green-up.

Aeration & Overseeding

That clay soil we mentioned? It compacts hard by the end of summer, choking off water and air to your grass roots. Fall core aeration punches through the compaction layer, and overseeding fills in the thin spots so your lawn comes back thicker the following spring. This is the single most impactful thing you can do for a Noblesville lawn each year.

Landscaping, Mulch & Everything Else

We're not just a mowing company. Mulch and rock installation, landscape bed renovations, hedge and shrub trimming, paver patios and driveways, spring and fall cleanups, leaf removal, snow removal, mosquito control. We handle it all, twelve months a year.

Your Year-Round Plan

What Your Noblesville Property Needs, Season by Season

Hamilton County's climate keeps your lawn and landscape on a year-round cycle. Here's how we keep Noblesville properties in top shape through every phase.

Spring (March through May)

Spring cleanup to clear winter debris, cut back dead perennials, and re-edge landscape beds. Pre-emergent weed control goes down when soil temperatures hit 55 degrees, usually early to mid-April in Noblesville. First mow of the season typically starts the third week of April, depending on how early the grass starts growing. Fresh mulch in the beds completes the spring reset.

Summer (June through August)

Weekly mowing at 3.75 to 4 inches to keep the turf healthy through the heat. Post-emergent weed treatments for crabgrass, clover, and dandelions that push through despite the pre-emergent. Mosquito control for properties near Morse Reservoir and the White River corridor. Shrub and hedge trimming to keep landscaping shaped and healthy. Watch for brown patch and dollar spot during humid stretches in July and August.

Fall (September through November)

This is the most important season for your lawn. Core aeration and overseeding in September or early October while soil is still warm. Winterizer fertilizer application to strengthen roots before dormancy. Leaf removal as the hardwoods drop, especially heavy on properties near Forest Park and along Pleasant Street. Fall cleanup to clear beds and prep the property for winter.

Winter (December through February)

Commercial snow plowing and ice management for parking lots and commercial properties. Trigger-depth system means we dispatch automatically when accumulation hits your threshold. Planning season for any landscape projects you want to tackle in spring.
