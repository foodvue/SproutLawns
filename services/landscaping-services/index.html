What We Offer

Our Landscaping Services

Every property is different. We offer a full range of landscaping services that can be tailored to your needs and budget.

Plantings & Landscape Bed Renovations

New shrubs, perennials, ornamental grasses, and seasonal flowers installed in fresh or renovated beds. We select plants that thrive in Indiana's climate and match the light, soil, and maintenance level you're looking for. Learn more about plantings.

Mulch & Rock Installation

Dyed mulch, shredded hardwood, and decorative rock ground covers installed in landscape beds. Mulch retains moisture, regulates soil temperature, and keeps weeds down. We haul, spread, and edge. You just enjoy the result. Learn more about mulch & rock.

Landscape Trimming & Pruning

Keep your shrubs, hedges, and small trees shaped and healthy with regular trimming and seasonal pruning. Overgrown landscaping doesn't just look messy. It blocks airflow and invites disease. Learn more about trimming & pruning.

Paver Patios & Driveways

Custom paver installations for patios, walkways, and driveways. Pavers add curb appeal and functional outdoor living space that lasts for decades with minimal maintenance. Learn more about pavers.

Sod Installation

When seeding isn't fast enough or the area is too damaged for overseeding, sod gives you an instant lawn. We prep the soil, lay the sod, and get you growing. Learn more about sod installation.

Seasonal Cleanups

Spring and fall cleanup services to clear debris, remove leaves, edge beds, and get your property ready for the next season. Learn more about cleanups.

Common Questions

Landscaping Services FAQ

Landscaping costs in Hamilton County vary widely depending on scope. A simple bed renovation or mulch refresh might run $500–$1,500, while a full landscape design and installation with plants, edging, and mulch can range from $3,000 to $15,000 or more. Hardscape projects like paver patios add to that. We provide free estimates so you know exactly what your specific project will cost.

Spring (April–May) and fall (September–October) are the best windows for landscaping in Indiana. Spring planting gives shrubs and perennials a full growing season to establish roots. Fall planting takes advantage of cooler temps and warm soil. Hardscape work like pavers and retaining walls can be done May through November.

In Hamilton County’s competitive real estate market, the highest-ROI landscaping projects are fresh mulch and bed edging, foundation plantings, a defined paver walkway or patio, and landscape lighting. The National Association of Realtors estimates a well-done landscape can recover 100% or more of its cost at sale. Even simple improvements dramatically improve curb appeal.

Yes. We handle everything from simple bed layout plans to full property landscape designs. Our process starts with an on-site consultation to understand your goals, budget, and the specific conditions of your property — sun exposure, drainage, soil type, and existing features. Hamilton County properties vary a lot, so we design with your specific site in mind.

For most standard landscaping work — plantings, mulch, bed edging — no permit is needed. However, some Hamilton County municipalities require permits for retaining walls over a certain height (typically 4 feet), significant grading changes, or structures near property lines. Carmel and Fishers also have HOA requirements that may dictate plant types or bed locations.

Small projects like mulching a few beds or planting annuals are manageable for most homeowners. But for anything involving grading, drainage, retaining walls, large plantings, or design work, a professional saves time and avoids costly mistakes. Indiana’s clay soil and freeze-thaw cycles make proper installation critical — plants set too deep or beds without drainage fail within a year.
